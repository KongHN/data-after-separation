Shadlen），《证据波动性对感知决策中的选择、反应时间和信心的影响》，《eLife》，第 5 卷（2016 年）：e17688。刺激因素：随机点运动图。固定的刺激持续时间为 200 毫秒。参与者必须判断运动的净方向是向右还是向左。在.csv 文件中，“刺激（Stimulus）” 字段表示运动的真实方向（0：向左；1：向右）。对于 0% 运动一致性的试验，导致获得正反馈的运动方向是随机选择的。信心度量表：在注视点两侧各呈现一个新月形目标。左侧（右侧）目标用于表示感知到的运动方向是向左（向右）。受试者被指示，如果他们对自己的决策完全确定，则选择目标的最上端（信心 = 1），如果他们的信心不高于猜测水平，则选择最下端（信心 = 0）。中间值代表中间的信心水平。操作变量：每次试验中，运动强度和运动波动性以伪随机方式变化。在.csv 文件中，“一致性（Coherence）” 字段表示运动强度（六种可能的值），“条件（Condition）” 字段表示波动性（0：低波动性，1：高波动性）。组块规模：96 次试验。每个受试者每天进行 4 - 6 个组块的试验，持续多天（约 4 天），直到每个参与者完成 1536 - 2107 次试验。反馈：每次测试后，参与者会收到听觉反馈，指示选择是正确还是错误。缺失值字段：如果受试者在运动期间未能保持注视，反应将被记录为缺失值（NaN）。受试者群体：年轻成年人（纽约哥伦比亚大学的研究生和本科生）。反应设备：眼动追踪仪和计算机键盘。实验目的：检验有界证据积累模型关于证据强度和波动性如何影响选择、反应时间和信心的预测。主要结果：噪声增加会导致决策更快且信心更高，尽管对准确性的影响可忽略不计，这与模型的预测一致。实验日期：数据收集于 2015 年 4 月至 2015 年 5 月之间。数据收集地点：美国纽约哥伦比亚大学的迈克・沙德伦实验室。数据收集语言：英语。类别：其他：决策制定。.csv 文件中的字段：“Subj_idx”：受试者识别编号（1 到 3）“Stimulus”：0：向左运动；1：向右运动。对于 0% 运动一致性的试验，此值随机选择。“Response”：0：选择左侧目标；1：选择右侧目标。“Confidence”：连续值，介于 0（信心不高于猜测水平）和 1（完全确定）之间。“RT_decConf”：运动刺激结束到参与者按下空格键输入其决策之间的时间。“Coherence”：一个点按运动方向移动而非随机移动的概率。“Condition”：0：低波动性；1：高波动性。“Accuracy”：0：错误的运动判断；1：正确的运动判断。“Block”：组块编号。“Date”：数据收集的日期（格式：yyymmdd）。ContributorAriel Zylberberg (ariel.zylberberg@gmail.com)CitationZylberberg, Ariel, Christopher R. Fetsch, and Michael N. Shadlen. "The influence of evidence volatility on choice, reaction time and confidence in a perceptual decision." Elife 5 (2016): e17688.StimulusRandom-dot kinematogram. Fixed stimulus duration of 200 ms. Participants had to decide if the net direction of motion was rightward or leftward. In the .csv file, the field 'Stimulus' indicates the true direction of motion (0: leftward; 1: rightward). For trials of 0% motion coherence, the direction of motion that led to positive feedback was selected at random. Confidence scaleTwo crescent-shaped targets presented on each side of the fixation. The left (right) target was used to indicate that the perceived direction of motion was to the left (right, respectively). Subjects were instructed to select the upper extreme of the targets if they were completely certain of their decision (Confidence = 1), and the lowermost extreme if their confidence was not higher than guessing (Confidence = 0). Intermediate values represent intermediate levels of confidence. ManipulationsMotion strength and motion volatility were varied pseudo-randomly from trial to trial.In the .csv file, the field 'Coherence' denotes the strength of motion (six possible values) and the field 'Condition' denotes volatility (0: low volatility, 1: high volatility).Block size96 trials. Each subject performed 4-6 blocks per day, over many days (~4) until completing 1536-2107 trials per participant.FeedbackAfter each test, participants received auditory feedback that indicated whether the choice was correct or incorrect.NaN fieldsResponses were recorded as NaN if subjects failed to maintain fixation during the motion epoch.Subject populationYoung adults (graduate and under-graduate students of Columbia University, NY).Response deviceEyetracker and computer keyboard.Experiment goalTest the predictions of a bounded evidence accumulation model for how evidence strength and volatility affect choice, reaction time and confidence. Main resultAn increase in noise leads to faster decisions and higher confidence despite negligible effects on accuracy, consistent with the model's prediction.Experiment datesData were collected between April, 2015 and May, 2015.Location of data collectionMike Shadlen lab at Columbia University, New York, USA.Language of data collectionEnglish.CategoryOther: Decision-making.--------------------------------------------Fields in the .csv file: 'Subj_idx'  : subject identification number (1 to 3)'Stimulus'  : 0: leftward motion; 1: rightward motion. Selected at random for trials of 0% motion coherence.'Response'  : 0: left-target chosen; 1: right-target chosen.'Confidence': continuous between 0 (confidence not higher than guessing) and 1 (full certainty).'RT_decConf': time between the offset of the motion stimulus and the moment at which the participant pressed the space-bar to input his or her decision.'Coherence' : probability that a dot is displaced in motion as opposed to randomly.'Condition' : 0: low volatility; 1: high volatility.'Accuracy'  : 0: incorrect motion judgment; 1: correct motion judgment.'Block'     : block number.'Date'      : day in which the data were collected (format: yyymmdd).
